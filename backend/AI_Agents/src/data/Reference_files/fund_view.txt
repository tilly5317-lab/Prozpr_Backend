Prozper MARKET OUTLOOK — MARCH 2026

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Indian equity markets continue to demonstrate resilience amid global uncertainties, with the Nifty 50 trading near historical highs. The domestic macroeconomic fundamentals remain supportive, backed by steady GDP growth, controlled inflation trajectory, and robust corporate earnings. However, valuations across market caps warrant a cautious but tactical approach, with a differentiated stance across asset classes reflecting both opportunity and prudence.

EQUITY MARKET OUTLOOK
Large-Cap Equities: NEUTRAL TO SELECTIVE ACCUMULATION
The large-cap segment (NIFTY 50, BSE Sensex) continues to dominate fund inflows and valuations have consolidated after the recent run-up. We maintain a neutral stance with selective accumulation on dips, particularly among defensive, dividend-paying large caps and quality financial services. The risk-reward for bulk large-cap indices appears balanced at current valuations, with earnings growth of 8-10% expected over the next 12 months supporting current price levels. Sectors: Banking remains overweight; pharmaceutical and consumer discretionary offer selective opportunities.

Mid-Cap Equities: OVERWEIGHT
Mid-cap equities (NIFTY MID CAP 100) offer superior earnings growth momentum at more reasonable valuations than mega-cap counterparts. We recommend overweighting mid-cap exposure, particularly in quality companies with strong balance sheets, sustainable competitive advantages, and double-digit earnings growth potential. The mid-cap space is attracting consistent fund flows as investors increasingly seek value. Short-term (0-6m) outlook: Positive, with potential for 12-15% returns; Medium-term (6-18m): Strong fundamentals support steady appreciation; Long-term (18m+): Mid-caps positioned for sustained outperformance.

Small-Cap Equities: UNDERWEIGHT WITH SELECTIVE EXPOSURE
Small-cap indices have experienced significant volatility; valuations remain stretched for many stocks despite recent corrections. We recommend underweighting small-cap exposure in most portfolios, restricting allocations only to highly-selective opportunities with proven business models and management quality. Market cap below 200 crore should be avoided unless addressing specific portfolio needs. Short-term: Expect consolidation; Medium-term: Selective opportunities emerging; Long-term: Structural growth potential for high-quality businesses.

DEBT/FIXED INCOME OUTLOOK
Yield Environment: DURATION NEUTRAL
The RBI is likely to maintain an accommodative stance over the next 6-12 months, with the repo rate expected to stay in the 6.25%-6.5% range. The 10-year government security yield is trading around 6.8%, offering reasonable risk-adjusted returns for medium-duration bonds. We recommend a neutral duration stance, avoiding excessive duration extension while not overlooking the opportunity in select corporate bonds. Credit spreads remain tight, so selective credit quality upgrades and high-grade corporate bonds offer value over government securities.

Bond Allocation: Mix of government securities (60%), AAA/AA-rated corporate bonds (30%), and short-duration instruments (10%) is recommended. Floating-rate instruments are also attractive given the potential for stable-to-rising yields in Q2-Q3 2026.

GOLD & PRECIOUS METALS OUTLOOK
Gold Outlook: UNDERWEIGHT
Global gold prices are presently elevated, supported by geopolitical tensions and central bank buying. However, the strength of the Indian rupee against the US dollar has capped rupee-denominated gold appreciation. We recommend an underweight stance on gold, restricting allocations to 10-15% of total portfolio for hedging purposes only. Domestic gold demand is expected to remain steady but not exceptional given current price levels.

Key considerations:
- International gold prices influenced by US Fed policy and geopolitical factors
- Rupee strength has capped domestic gold returns; expect volatility
- Gold serving as portfolio stabilizer rather than growth driver

ASSET CLASS STANCES
Equity (Large+Mid+Small Cap): OVERWEIGHT at 60-65% for moderate risk profiles
Debt (Bonds & Fixed Income): HOLD at 25-30%
Gold: UNDERWEIGHT at 5-10%

TIMEFRAME ANALYSIS
Short-Term (0-6 Months): Markets expected to trade in a range with periodic profit-taking; mid-caps to outperform; maintain diversified exposure across caps.

Medium-Term (6-18 Months): Earnings growth to drive market appreciation; rotation towards quality and value expected; debt expected to provide stability; gold to remain neutral.

Long-Term (18+ Months): Indian equities positioned for sustained growth driven by structural tailwinds (demographic dividend, digital transformation, consumption growth); allocation strategy should emphasize quality and diversification across market caps.

SECTORAL RECOMMENDATIONS
Overweight: Financial Services (Banks, NBFCs), Pharmaceuticals
Neutral: IT Services, Fmcg, Utilities
Underweight: Oil & Gas, Telecom (expensive valuations)

CONCLUSION
The Prozper allocation strategy for March 2026 emphasizes a balanced, diversified approach with selective overweighting to mid-cap equities and quality fixed income. Risk management and quality remain paramount given current market valuations, with gold serving a hedging rather than growth role. Investors are advised to rebalance portfolios quarterly and maintain discipline amid market fluctuations.
